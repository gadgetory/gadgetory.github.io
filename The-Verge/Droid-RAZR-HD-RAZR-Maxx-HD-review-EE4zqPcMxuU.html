Droid RAZR HD + RAZR Maxx HD review

2012-10-22
well this is Dan with the verge and we're looking at the motorola droid razr HD and maxx HD which are now available from Verizon Wireless the new phones look really familiar they're very similar in appearance to last year's razr and razr maxx right down to their sharp rectangular corners and excessive border around the new 720p display as you can see the razr HD into max are really similar to each other the max is slightly heavier and even more slightly thicker but it still provides a thirty three percent larger battery and double the internal storage of the standard razr HD overall the hardware is slightly less impressive than last year's razors the general shape of the phone doesn't mask its large size as well as phones from HTC and Samsung do which makes a razor HD feel noticeably larger than the competition and the angular edges and abundance of Kevlar in the back of the phone aren't very attractive but what is new and improved here is the display which has been upgraded from an unimpressive 4.3 inch qhd Super AMOLED unit to a new 4.7 inch 720p HD Super AMOLED screen the new display is much sharper than before it's really bright and it has great viewing angles but once you dig a bit deeper into it the cracks start to show like other Motorola displays the razr HD screen is extremely saturated and it also carries a very bluish cast which makes White's look really inaccurate software wise the razr HD is unfortunately launching with Android 4.0 Ice Cream Sandwich though both motorola and verizon have promised that an upgrade to 4.1 jellybean will be available before the end of this year the razr HD features the same minimal modifications to Ice Cream Sandwich that we saw on the razr m and they are pretty much limited to new icons a modified launcher with quick settings available to left of the home screen and a revised lockscreen with shortcuts for camera phone and messages inside the razr HD we find a dual-core 1.5 gigahertz Qualcomm Snapdragon s4 processor and it performs just as well here as we've seen in other smartphones for the most part day-to-day activities are fast and responsive apps open quickly and switching between them is very easy and it has no trouble running even 3d games but at the end of the day the razr HD and razr HD max don't really bring anything new to the table that we haven't seen from other Android manufacturers already if Motorola's design is your cup of tea we strongly recommend just going with a max version since it provides significantly longer battery than the regular model without a huge difference in size or weight you can pick up the razr HD from Verizon Wireless right now for 199 on contract and you can also get the razr maxx HD for 299 on contract of course with Google expected to announce a number of new index smartphones in the very near future it might be best to just wait a few weeks if you possibly can
