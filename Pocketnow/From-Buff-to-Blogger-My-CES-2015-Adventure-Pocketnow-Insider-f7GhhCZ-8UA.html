From Buff to Blogger: My CES 2015 Adventure (Pocketnow Insider)

2015-01-17
CES has always been a dream of mine I've always wanted to just drop everything for a week and fly to Vegas to check out all the new crazy gadgets and cars and tech that the new year has to offer but part of the fun of seeing everything is telling all of your friends about it when it's over taking them through the experience my name is hi out to human and I want to take you through the experience of covering CES with pocket now the first day I landed in Vegas after I had checked into my hotel and picked up my badge I dove into the rental car captained by mr. two phones himself and our journey began we tackled LG and sony early on in the day and the slow pace of day zero gave me time to learn how the pocket now CES machine would operate every morning after that Michael Jaime and I would find a place to meet up and head to the convention center where we go over the plans for the day and act accordingly that usually meant that Michael and I would go cover a device while Jaime edited previous footage in the press room I learned very quickly to appreciate the rental car we had which made getting across town for meetings much easier at least when we could find a place to park thank you very much recovering devices and editing videos is only half of CES or any trade show really the other half is meeting people and having a good time sure you might run into some familiar faces emits too hurried power walk to the next device but it's good to have a place where everyone can just unwind and socialize showstoppers was just that place and man were there some awesome people there and awesome food we got the chance to check out a few booths while we were there one of which featured the yotaphone too and to our surprise we were even given a review unit on the spot it was a great time of course not everything can go smoothly at CES and for us that was both thanks to time constraints and the miscommunications you see the waterfall that's where I want to film our goodbyes chose the podcast instead all things considered it was a fairly seamless experience from start to finish and we had just enough time left over by the end to visit friend of the show nicole scott and record an onsite podcast to close out our final night of coverage before saying goodbye it may not be the best trade show for mobile the CES 2015 was the best time I've had in a long while and it's all thanks to Michael hi May and the rest of the guys from pocket now so be sure to subscribe and follow the guys on the ground for Jaime Rivera I auto Houston and all pocket now I'm Michael Fisher we'll see you next time you are so much better than I am
