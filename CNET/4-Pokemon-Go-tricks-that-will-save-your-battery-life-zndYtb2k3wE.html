4 Pokemon Go tricks that will save your battery life

2016-07-15
if you've been playing a Pokemon go for a while you've probably noticed your batteries been taking somewhat of a hit and unless you're lugging around one of these bricks you may get stuck in an epic gym battle with no power and no outlets in sight make sure you're getting the most out of each charge with these battery saving tips number one turn on the battery saver the app comes with a built-in battery saving feature and since it doesn't affect the game there's no reason why you shouldn't have it on all the time tap on the pokeball on the bottom of the screen tap on settings now take the box beside the battery saver option next try Google maps offline Pokemon Go uses Google Maps to pinpoint your location within the app so by downloading the map ahead of time you'll conserve juice and data as you go first open the Google Maps app select the settings menu and tap on offline areas press the plus sign and pinch the screen to select the area you want now hit download now try turning off AR the augmented reality features a fun way to interact with your environment but it also uses the phone's camera which is another battery hog first you need to find a Pokemon once you targeted you'll see an AR toggle appear on the top of the screen now slide the toggle to the left and you'll no longer see the street in front of you and the Pokemon will actually be easier to trap and lastly try lowering the brightness of your screen this one is more of a last resort move because trying to catch Pokemon in broad daylight can be tough with a dim screen go to your display settings or just slide to view your shortcuts turn off auto brightness and adjust
