How to clean your oven without harsh chemicals (CNET How To)

2017-11-18
the inside of your oven is probably a lot dirtier than you'd like to admit fortunately a clean oven is just a few steps away and you don't even need harsh chemicals or a self-cleaning cycle to do so here's what you'll need dish gloves baking soda vinegar dish soap water a spray bottle a sponge or cleaning cloth paper towels a scraper or spatula in a large bowl or bucket first remove the racks from your oven and wash them in warm soapy water dry the racks and set them aside next remove any loose food particles from your oven then make a paste using three parts baking soda and one part water spread the paste around the inside of your oven if there are really tough burnt on spots put a little extra baking soda paste on them close your oven and let the paste sit overnight next moisten your sponge or cleaning cloth and warm water and wipe away the baking soda paste for a tea stubborn messes use a scraper or core spatula next fill a spray bottle with one part vinegar and one part water spray down the oven and wipe away leftover baking soda paste with paper towel if they're still baking soda and some of the ovens crevices use a vacuum to clear them away lastly replace the racks and enjoy your nice clean oven
