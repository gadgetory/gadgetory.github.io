The Panasonic E50 LCD TV - First Look

2012-07-06
hi i'm ty pendlebury from cnet and this is a review of the panasonic e 50 television ever since pioneer pulled out at the television race there's been one name on the top of the heap when it comes to plasmas panasonic but unfortunately plasmas don't sell as well as LCDs or the sexiest sounding LEDs and so the company is branched out to offer the customer more choice the e 50 series sits at the budget end of Panasonic's line and for goes some of the high-end features such as 3d and local dimming however it still has smart TV with easy access to netflix and the like however it's a wired connection only unless you buy a wireless dongle for between fifty and a hundred dollars style-wise it's okay with a piano black bezel which should fit most rooms and it's relatively slim as well despite its budget nature the TV does offer for HDMI ports and an SD card slot for viewing pictures picture quality though is disappointing with colors that are just a little bit to read and with black levels that are among some of the worst available at the price if I was wanting to spend around a thousand dollars on the TV I'd skip over this one completely and get either Panasonic's own UT 50 plasma or the toshiba 5200 LCD this has been ty pendlebury for CNN com you
