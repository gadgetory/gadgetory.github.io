Google bets big on hardware with HTC deal (CNET News)

2017-09-20
Google wants to be a big name in hardware and it's paying 1.1 billion dollars to get there the company announced a partnership with HTC on Wednesday to get access to talent from the HTC team many of whom are already working on Google's pixel phones Google will also license HTC's intellectual property under the deal but Google stopped short of an anticipated buyout of the Taiwanese smartphone company perhaps indicating that it's not quite ready to make the kind of big investments it's made in Hardware in the past five years ago the company bought Motorola Mobility for twelve point five billion dollars only to sell it at a loss less than two years later but Google is no doubt hoping the partnership with HTC will be different after all HTC was the manufacturer behind Google's flagship pixel smartphones in a market dominated by Apple and Samsung these handsets helped Google win new fans in the premium smartphone space in a statement today Google's senior vice president of hardware Rick ostilow said the company was building on 10 years of history with HTC and investing in one team for the long run so what's next for Google well we won't have to wait very long to find out they're unveiling their next generation of products on October 4th
