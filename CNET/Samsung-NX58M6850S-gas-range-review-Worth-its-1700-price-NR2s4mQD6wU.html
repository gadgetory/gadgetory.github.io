Samsung NX58M6850S gas range review: Worth its $1,700 price

2017-11-14
at first glance nothing is exceptional about this 1699 Samsung freestanding gas oven especially when you compare it to other ovens it doesn't boil food super fast it takes its sweet time to boil burgers too and it's one of a relatively few number of ranges that connects to your Wi-Fi network so you can control some functions through an app but connectivity was just an extra feature not essential so why did I end up falling in love with this oven because each of its individual features and performance results added up to a great overall product yes it's not the fastest boiler but it's far from the slowest we've ever tested and yes I got antsy waiting for the Samsung to broil burgers but the end result was a batch of the juicy patties that made up for the wait the best feature of this oven is its Flex duo insert when you put this panel in the oven it creates two separate cooking cavities so you can cook let's say a casserole on a chicken at different temperatures in the same oven and when you need the whole oven for something big like a turkey you can just remove the Flex duo until you're ready to use it again I appreciate this flexibility especially as oven use kicks up into high gear over the holidays at nearly 1700 dollars the samsung range is more than you typically expect for a free-standing gas stove but it's dependable cooking performance and Flex duo insert make this product a worthwhile investment for your kitchen
