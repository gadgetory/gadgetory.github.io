CNET How To - Use the HTC One M8 as a fitness tracker

2014-04-01
I'm Dan Graziano and I'm here with a brand new HTC One m8 if you watched any of my earlier videos you probably notice my fitbit force I wear this thing all the time it's one of my favorite fitness trackers but what does this have to do with this well when you buy the new HTC One you won't have to go out and spend extra money on a fitness tracker similar to the iphone 5s and its m7 chip the HTC One m8 includes a low-power motion sensor that is capable of tracking basic health and fitness activity to set it up download the free Fitbit app from the Google Play Store if you already have a Fitbit account simply login if not you must create an account when you're asked what fitness tracker you'd like to set up select no Fitbit yet and then click on set up your phone after entering your height gender weight birthday and other personal information you're ready to go the HTC One m8 will now automatically track your steps distance travelled and calories burned which can then be viewed on the Fitbit app or the company's website when you reach certain milestones such as gaining fifteen thousand steps in a day Fitbit award you with badges which can then be viewed on the Fitbit website on the HTC One m8 however these badges are displayed right on your blinkfeed home screen for more how tunes like this one be sure to visit how to cnet com if you have questions or comments feel free to reach out to me on twitter i'm dan graziano cnet thanks for watching you
