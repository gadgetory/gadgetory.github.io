Hands-on with the familiar metal curves of the HTC 10

2016-04-12
HTC has got a brand new super phone and it's called to the HTC ten physically you can see that this is a quite similar beast to the previous models it's got this curving all-metal bat it's got a 5.2 inch display on the front there's a fingerprint sensor down at the bottom and here's something new it's got USB type-c port on the bottom now this runs on the latest Android marshmallow software but HTC has made sure it's a lot more simple to use it's got rid of some of its own native apps using Google's apps instead so you don't have to decide which email client or which phone or contact client you want to use it does make the software look nice and neat right out of the box but there are a whole load of themes you can choose to personalize the look of the phone pretty much as much as you want HTC has been talking a lot about the camera on the back of the phone it's got a 12 ultra pixel camera which apparently uses larger pixels to bring in more light it's also got a wide aperture lens all of which should hopefully mean that your low-light shots look better than ever the rear camera can also record video in 4k resolution and around on the front you'll find a 5 megapixel camera which is apparently the first front facing camera to have optical image stabilization other features include 32 gigabytes of on-board storage support for micro SD cards there's also a battery that HTC thinks will last for two days and it will get half of its charge back with only 30 minutes of charging time there's sadly no word on price as of yet but we do expect to hear more in the coming weeks as this phone is due to go on sale globally in May
