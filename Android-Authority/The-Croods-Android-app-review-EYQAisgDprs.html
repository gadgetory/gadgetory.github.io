The Croods- Android app review!

2013-03-16
hello ladies and gentlemen i'm joe hindi the android authority app guy after the intro we're gonna be taking a closer look at the Croods the Croods is based on a movie that will be hitting theaters March 22nd 2013 it has some pretty big stars like Nicolas Cage Cloris Leachman Bryan Reynolds and Emma Watson the game itself is a collaborative effort between the well-known creator of Angry Birds Rovio along with the widely popular animation studio DreamWorks if you are going into this hoping for another addictive yet repetitive level based game like Angry Birds you're going to be disappointed in reality the Croods plays more like your standard Facebook flash game this includes the time constraints when performing actions having access to in app purchases and the gradual unlocks of new features items and areas the premise of the game is that you need to capture wild prehistoric animals you then need to tame them build them homes and feed them when you feed them they produce new items that you can then use to build more things this dynamic continues indefinitely as there's always animals to feed and things to build in addition you complete chores given to you by gran which earns you some experience and coins usually they're pretty simple and involve capturing animals removing trees feeding animals or collecting items you unlock more chores as you progress through the game and gain levels if you find that you have a boatload of items that you just can't unload Buga will make you soup in exchange for raw items the soup can be used to upgrade animals and there's a darn fine handy way to grind for gold coins the graphics are on par with most Facebook games which is nice because those games are typically played on a PC and this is an Android game the style of graphics is classic DreamWorks and you'll have no trouble discerning which characters are which from the movie the soundtrack is very meth and that's really all we can say about it moving around the game is usually pretty smooth unless you have an older Android device there are animations to show that things are happening and the people do move around some but it's nothing groundbreaking overall it wasn't a visually impressive experience but it did look better than average grabbing this game from the Google Play Store is free beware though as it is a freemium title so you can purchase items like gold coins and crystals with real-life money you can play through the game without being required to buy these items but it is very tedious and we'll definitely take longer to get to the later parts of the game this is actually our least favorite mechanic in the game and many reviewers on the Google Play Store agree the game is pretty fun and addictive when you start out but once you run low on coins and crystals the game slows to a new halt unless you spend cash the game quickly becomes a grind for coins and crystals well it doesn't stop it from being fun at first tedious grinding for the same coins and crystals gets tiresome after a while if you're a fan of Facebook flash games like Farmville or cafe world then you should feel right at home with the Croods however more serious gamers will be immediately irritated once they get to the point where it's just a tedious grind for gold and crystal the best we can say about it is that at least they don't make you pay to download the app overall this game showed a lot of promise after all the Angry Birds franchise has over a billion downloads and DreamWorks is generally considered to be a positive force in the movie industry to that end the gameplay does have the potential to be addicting and it is a good-looking game however the game is severely bottlenecked as players will have to resource grind for copious amounts of time just to advance unless they want to shell out a lot of money if these kind of titles are your thing you'll definitely love the Croods if you're a more serious gamer then you may want to look
